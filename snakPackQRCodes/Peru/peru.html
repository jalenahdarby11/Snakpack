General Information

Country Name: Republic of Peru (República del Perú)

Capital: Lima

Official Language: Spanish (Quechua and Aymara also recognized)

Currency: Peruvian Sol (PEN)

Population: Approx. 34 million

Time Zone: UTC -5:00

Geography

Location: Western South America, bordered by Ecuador, Colombia, Brazil, Bolivia, Chile, and the Pacific Ocean

Area: 1,285,216 square kilometers (496,225 square miles)

Climate: Ranges from arid coastal plains to Andean highlands and Amazon rainforest; varies greatly by region

Government

Type: Unitary Presidential Republic

President: Dina Boluarte (as of 2025)

Legislature: Unicameral Congress of the Republic

Independence: July 28, 1821 (from Spain)

Economy

GDP: $268 billion USD (nominal, 2022)

Major Industries: Mining (gold, copper), agriculture, fishing, textiles, and tourism

Currency Exchange Rate: 1 USD ≈ 3.8 PEN (approximate, may vary)

Tourism & Attractions

Popular Destinations: Machu Picchu, Cusco, Sacred Valley, Lake Titicaca, Nazca Lines, and the Amazon rainforest

Cultural Heritage: Famous for Inca history, vibrant festivals, and traditional Andean music and textiles

Fun Facts

National Motto: "Firme y feliz por la unión" (Steady and happy for the union)

Famous For: Machu Picchu, ceviche, llamas and alpacas, ancient civilizations, and breathtaking mountain landscapes

Life Expectancy: Around 77 years

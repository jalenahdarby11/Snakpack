General Information

Country Name: Republic of Costa Rica

Capital: San José

Official Language: Spanish

Currency: Costa Rican Colón (CRC)

Population: Approx. 5.1 million

Time Zone: UTC -6:00 (Central Standard Time)

Geography

Location: Central America, bordered by Nicaragua to the north, Panama to the south, the Pacific Ocean to the west, and the Caribbean Sea to the east.

Area: 51,100 square kilometers (19,700 square miles)

Climate: Tropical, with distinct wet and dry seasons. Coastal areas are hot and humid, while inland regions can be cooler.

Government

Type: Democratic Republic

President: Rodrigo Chaves Robles

Legislature: Unicameral Legislative Assembly

Independence: September 15, 1821 (from Spain)

Economy

GDP: $66.9 billion USD (nominal, 2022)

Major Industries: Electronics, medical devices, tourism, agriculture (coffee, bananas, pineapples), and renewable energy.

Currency Exchange Rate: 1 USD ≈ 600 CRC (approximate, may vary)

Tourism & Attractions

Popular Destinations: Arenal Volcano, Manuel Antonio National Park, Monteverde Cloud Forest, Tortuguero National Park, and the beaches of the Pacific Coast.

Wildlife: Known for its biodiversity, Costa Rica is home to a wide variety of animals including sloths, monkeys, toucans, and sea turtles.

Fun Facts

National Motto: "Pura Vida" (Pure Life) - a phrase that reflects the Costa Rican way of life, focusing on happiness, simplicity, and enjoying life.

Famous For: Being a world leader in environmental conservation and sustainability, with a large percentage of its land protected as national parks and reserves.

Life Expectancy: One of the highest in the world, with an average of 80 years.

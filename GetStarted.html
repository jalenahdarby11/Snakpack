Costa Rica

In Costa Rica, snacks like chips and pretzels are enjoyed in various forms, with local ingredients and flavors giving them a unique twist. Tostones, crispy twice-fried slices of green plantains, are a popular alternative to regular chips, often served with a sprinkle of salt or garlic. Yuca chips, made from cassava, offer a denser, crunchier alternative to traditional potato chips, while chifles, thinly sliced fried plantains, are a light, savory snack commonly found in local markets. While plantains and yuca are central to Costa Rican snack culture, traditional potato chips are also widely available, often in familiar flavors like salted or lime.

Chad

In Chad, snacks often reflect the country's diverse culinary traditions, blending local ingredients and flavors in simple yet delicious ways. One of the most popular snacks is fritters (boules de farine), which are deep-fried dough balls made from wheat flour, sugar, and sometimes a bit of vanilla or cinnamon. Another popular snack is koki, a savory dish made from black-eyed peas or beans that are steamed in banana leaves, resulting in a dense, flavorful, and protein-packed snack. Chips (fries) are also common in Chad, usually made from fresh potatoes, sliced thin and fried until crispy.
